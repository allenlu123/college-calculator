MIT is a school in Cambridge, Massachusetts, which is 3 miles away from Boston. It was founded in 1861, it is located in an urban setting, and its size is 168 acres. MIT is known for its emphasis in STEM majors, with its top five major choices being engineering, computer science, physical sciences, biological and biomedical sciences, and mathematics. Its two most prestigious graduate schools are its School of Engineering and the Sloan School of Management. MIT has a 4-1-4 academic schedule, in which the 1 month away from classes is used for research opportunities or internships. The student-faculty ratio is 8:1.
